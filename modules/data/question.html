Sex-limited traits are different from sex-influenced traits in that:

Select one:

a. sex-limited traits are inherited on the X-chromosome

b. sex-influenced traits are inherited on the X-chromosome

c. sex-limited traits have higher penetrance than sex-influenced traits

d. sex-influenced traits have higher penetrance than sex-limited traits

e. none of the above

      Ans. Sex-influenced trait: An autosomal trait influenced by the type of sex is called sex-influenced. Both the sexes may exhibit the trait, but the extent of phenotype expression is largely dependent of the type of sex. Such traits are generally autosomal recessive. These traits are present in both the sexes but their relative expression depends on the type of the sex.

      Sex-limited trait- A trait that is exhibited by one sex (say, male) but absent in the other sex is called sex-limited trait. These traits regulated the differential expression of primary and secondary sexual characters in either sexes under the influence of sex hormones; because each sex produces different set of hormones. The traits are generally located on autosomes and but can also be on sex-chromosomes.

      In other words, a trait is said to be sex-limited (i.e. only one sex has it) if it has zero penetrance if the opposite sex i.e. not present in the other sex.

      Thus, the correct answer is-

      d. Sex-influenced traits have higher penetrance than sex-limited traits.
